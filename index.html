Other Organizations

The Coalition to Stop Gun Violence: Founded in 1974, the Coalition to Stop Gun Violence (CSGV) seeks a future free from gun violence by advocating for stronger gun laws and fostering a national conversation about gun violence prevention. They approach gun violence as a public health issue, striving to reduce firearms access for criminals, terrorists, and those at risk of harming themselves or others. The CSGV focuses on areas such as banning assault weapons, limiting high-capacity ammunition magazines, and promoting safe storage of firearms. They also emphasize understanding and addressing the intersections of gun violence with mental illness and domestic abuse. Their multi-faceted approach places them at the forefront of the fight against gun violence.

Brady United Against Gun Violence: Brady United, formerly known as the Brady Campaign, is one of the oldest and most influential organizations fighting against gun violence in America. They've played a crucial role in advocating for and implementing life-saving gun policies at both the state and federal level. The organization is named after James Brady, who was permanently disabled as a result of an assassination attempt on President Ronald Reagan. Brady United's mission centers around three key strategies: creating a safer America by cutting gun deaths in half by 2025; expanding background checks to all gun sales; and changing the cultural perception of gun ownership. Their commitment to research, education, and legal advocacy makes them a vital player in the fight against gun violence.

Philadelphia CeaseFire is a public health program at the Center for Bioethics, Urban Health, and Policy at the Lewis Katz School of Medicine at Temple University. It uses the Cure Violence model, an evidence-based public health approach to prevent shootings and killings. This program treats gun violence as a public health epidemic and involves interrupting potential shootings, identifying and treating high-risk individuals, and changing social norms.
